Concept and Design

As the separation between indoors and outdoors becomes increasingly blurred, outdoor spaces identify new lifestyles, private and public, in harmony with nature. The Secret Garden collection of designer outdoor furniture is designed to adapt dining and relaxation areas to the most diverse contexts: from verandas to winter gardens, from private terraces or the outdoors of spas and wellness centres to the decks of yachts and ships. The Secret Garden, part of Poltrona Frau's Boundless Living Outdoor Collections, is a collection of outdoor furnishing with an elegant and sophisticated style, characterised by the painstaking workmanship of solid teak in a natural finish with organic and tapered lines. Designed by Roberto Lazzeroni, it offers outdoor solutions with attention to detail that merges Scandinavian taste with Made in Italy design.

The Secret Garden fulfils contemporary needs and an increasingly free use of outdoor furnishings. It has been designed for three functions: to furnish the relaxation area, with comfortable loungers and small tables, to create a comfortable dining area, thanks to the large table and armchairs, and for a more informal lounge dining area, where you can sit on the two-seater sofa or on the comfortable armchairs, with the large low table at your fingertips. The curved contours of the wood, the soft to the touch fabric covering the cushions, and the delicate tones of the hand-finished enamelled stoneware on the table tops are all elements featuring exquisite workmanship, a level of quality and manufacturing expertise rarely found in outdoor furnishings.

Designed by
Roberto Lazzeroni

Roberto Lazzeroni was born in Pisa. He studied art and architecture in Florence, taking an increasing personal interest in the phenomena of conceptual art and radical design during his years of training.He débuted professionally in the early 1980s with a series of important 'Interior Architecture' works that were immediately noticed and published by both Italian and international magazines. Roberto Lazzeroni has enjoyed an extremely dynamic professional journey that today sees him involved in various projects, and able to boast prestigious collaborations, art direction and interior and contract design in various parts of the world.Awards include: Tecniques Discretes (Paris, 1991), Conran Foundation Museum, (London, 1992), Elegant Techniques, (Chicago, 1992), La Fabbrica Estetica, (Paris, 1993), Dall'Albergo alla Nave, (Genoa, 1993), Costruire il mobile, (Milan 1999).
